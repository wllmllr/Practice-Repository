Running A Report For A Student Group

As the leader of the robotics team at Anderson Middle School, you recently used Schoolnet for PowerSchool to select your team members and create a student group. Now you need to pick a team captain. You know from experience that a successful robotics team captain will be a strong performer in language arts, science, and math. Instead of looking up each student’s test scores individually, is there a way to view test results for the whole team?

The answer is to use Schoolnet to view a student analysis report, filtered by your student group.

On the navigation bar, click Classrooms.

Then from the Course/Section menu, select Math 8 (2080) - All Sections.

To run a report for your student group, click Student Analysis.

Check Run Report Using Student Group. Then select Robotics Club from the menu.

Under Report Name, you see a list of the reports your school administrator has published in Schoolnet. You can run any of these reports filtered by any of your student groups. Since you want to find out how well your robotics team members have performed on their recent tests, click Student Performance Indicators.

The report shows math and language arts test results for the six students in your robotics club. It also shows the total number of days each student has been absent so far this year. But what about your students’ performance in science?

You can customize this report and add a column to show science test results. Click Advanced: edit this report.

Next, define the column of information you want to add to the student analysis report. The Column(s) Selected area lists the columns that are already defined for this report. To add another column that shows your students’ benchmark test results for science, select Benchmark from the Analyze By menu.

Then from the Subjects menu, select Life and Physical Sciences. Click Add Column to add the new column to your student analysis report.

That should give you all the information you need right now, so under Finished Defining? click Go to Spreadsheet.

The column you just defined has been added as the last column of your report. You review your students’ test results and are pleased to see that they are all solid performers in language arts, math, and science. But one student stands out across the board. That settles it. You decide to talk to Mark about being the new team captain!

Remember, to run a report on a student group in Schoolnet, follow these steps:

On the navigation bar, click Classrooms, then click the Student Analysis tab. Check Run Report Using Student Group and select a student group from the menu. Select a report and view the results.
